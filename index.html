Unraveling the Pandemic: A COVID-19 Journey Across the U.S.

This visualization page aims to narratively show the trend of covid-19 cases and deaths within U.S and the severity of each state as of specific time. The data comes from the covid-19-date puclished by New York Time, including the cumulative national cases and deaths over time, and the state-wise cases and deaths as of each date. The start date of the data is 2020-01-21, and as the time of publishing this visuzlization page, the end date in the data is 2023-03-23.

Cases Trend Over Time

The following chart represents the trend of covid-19 cases over time. Have your mouse hover over the line you'll be able to see the detail on demnd.

As visualized by the blue line chart above, the overall trend of the line is concave up and continuesly become steeper, which means that the infection is not under controlled for the most of the time. However, there's also some noticable elbow point that may convey interesting information, for example, for the first half of the 2021, the trend indicates that the infecton speed somehow becomes slower. And there is also a large elbow point at 2022-02 that turns the trend from fast increase to gradully slow down till the end.

Deaths Trend Over Time

The following chart represents the trend of covid-19 deaths over time. Have your mouse hover over the line you'll be able to see the detail on demnd.

According to the death trend above, different the previous cases trend, the overall trend of the deaths is concave up, which means that the mortality rate was becoming lower and lower in contrast with the faster speed of infection. As you may notice, it is interesting that the noticable elbow point on the deaths trend is similar to the cases trend. In the first half of the 2021, the lower slope indicates that the death speed somehow becomes slower. And a large elbow point at 2022-02 that turns the death trend from fast increase to gradully slow down till the end.

Cases and Death Distribution Across States

The following charts represents the distribution of covid-19 cases (blue) and deaths (red) across U.S states at a specific time. Have your mouse hover over the bar and you'll be able to see the detail on demnd. The default date represented by the chart is 2022-01-01, which is the time the cases and death increase substantially so that it is a good representitive of the distribution of the pandemic. There is also date selection bar, feel free to select other dates to explore the changes of the distribution

According to the bar charts above, it is noticable that the state with large population and maybe more travel attractive always have more cases and way mroe deaths compared to other states. For example, California, Florida, New York, and Texas have way more cases and death of all time. Recall that the date range of this Visualization is from 2020-1-21 to 2023-03-23, if you select dates in this range, you'll notice that height change of the bars are significate at the beginning, and varie less and less in the later time, which is basically consistant with the trend over time above.
